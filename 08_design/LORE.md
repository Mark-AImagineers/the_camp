# The Camp
### *A story of what remains*

---

Nobody remembers exactly when it started.

That's the thing nobody tells you — when the world ends, it doesn't announce itself. There's no single morning where everything was fine and then suddenly wasn't. It came in pieces. A news cycle that stopped refreshing. A neighbor who didn't come home. A sound from somewhere in the city that didn't sound like anything you'd heard before, and you told yourself it was construction, traffic, an animal, anything.

By the time people understood what was happening, it was already too late to do anything about it.

The ones who survived the first weeks did so mostly by accident — wrong place, wrong time, wrong instincts that turned out to be right. They hid when others ran. They ran when others hid. They kept their mouths shut when others called for help that wasn't coming.

Four years later, the ones still alive don't talk about how they made it. Not in detail. Not honestly. Survival is a private thing, and most of what people did to survive isn't something you repeat out loud.

---

## What Happened

No one knows. Not fully. Not anymore.

The pieces exist — in half-burned documents, in what the dying said before they died, in the way certain people go quiet when certain questions get asked. But no one has assembled those pieces into something complete, and the people who once had that picture seem to have gone out of their way to make sure it stays incomplete.

What survivors agree on: something changed in people. Not everyone. Not all at once. But people began to change, and the change moved, and the things they became were no longer the people they had been.

The word *infected* got used early on. Then *compromised*. Then people stopped using clinical language altogether because clinical language implies someone somewhere is working on a solution, and it became clear, eventually, that no one was.

Most people just call them **the Altered**. Or they don't call them anything. They just say *them*, and everyone in the room understands.

---

## The Altered

They were people.

That's the part that doesn't go away, no matter how long it's been. You see a face sometimes, in the Gray Zones, in the dead corridors of what used to be places — and you recognize it. A teacher. A woman from a market stall. Someone's father.

They don't act like people anymore. But they don't always act like animals either.

There are things that don't add up — things that get passed around in whispers between settlements, dismissed as fear or bad memory, because the alternative is worse. A door found closed from the inside in a building believed fully Altered for two years. A scavenging party that reported being followed for three kilometers through an industrial block — followed but not attacked. Markings on walls that no surviving human has claimed.

Nobody investigates. Nobody wants the answer badly enough to go looking for it.

---

## The World That Remained

The cities are shells. Some of them burned. Some of them didn't, which in some ways is worse — they just sit there, intact, full of everything you need and everything that wants to kill you.

Beyond the cities, the world grew back over the roads and the fences and the things people built to divide land from land. It doesn't care about borders. It doesn't care about ownership. It just grows.

Survivors organized themselves the way people always do — around safety, around food, around whoever seemed to know what they were doing. Some of those organizations became something worth calling a community. Others became something else. The difference between a settlement and a threat often comes down to how they treat strangers, and how hungry they are.

Resources are everything now. Food. Medicine. Fuel. Clean water. The people who have them set the terms of every conversation, every trade, every alliance. The people who don't have them do what they have to do.

---

## The Camp

It started as a warehouse.

Agricultural supply distribution, back when such things existed — high ceilings, concrete walls, a loading dock facing a road that doesn't see much traffic anymore. Someone stopped here because they were exhausted and the walls were solid and the doors could be secured. Someone else found them. Then another. Then more.

No one founded it. No one declared it anything. It accumulated.

Thirty people, roughly, depending on the week. Some have been here since close to the beginning. Some arrived last month. A few are children, which means decisions get made differently than they would otherwise — slower, more careful, with more weight on outcomes that used to feel abstract.

It has a name now. Someone spray-painted **THE CAMP** on the outer wall during the first weeks, half as a joke, a small defiance against the scale of everything that had gone wrong. Nobody painted over it. It went on the maps that get drawn and redrawn as territory shifts. It went into the mouths of traders from other settlements. Now it's just what it is.

What it is, is fragile. Everyone knows it. Nobody says it.

---

## You

Eleven days ago, the Coordinator left on an expedition.

They haven't come back.

No body. No message. No signal of any kind — just a team that went out and a silence where they used to be. The Camp waited. The Camp is still waiting, in the way that people wait when they've already begun to accept something they're not ready to say out loud.

In the meantime, someone has to hold the ledger. Someone has to answer when people ask what the plan is. Someone has to make the calls that keep thirty people fed and alive and pointed in a direction that isn't despair.

That someone is you.

You didn't ask for this. You weren't trained for it. You don't have a clear picture of what the previous Coordinator knew, what deals they'd made, what warnings they'd left half-written in a notebook you're still trying to decipher.

You have a set of keys. You have a half-finished inventory list. You have thirty people who are pretending to be less scared than they are because you're in the room and they need to believe someone has a grip on things.

Figure it out.

---

*The Camp does not explain itself. Neither does the world outside it.*
*You learn by doing. You pay for mistakes in the currency that matters now.*
*Some of the people here will die. Some will leave. Some will surprise you.*
*The only thing that keeps this place alive is the decision, made again every single day, to keep trying.*
