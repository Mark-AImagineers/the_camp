# The Mysteries
### *Designer bible. Internal only. The player never sees this.*

---

This document is the source of truth behind everything left unanswered in the game. Every cryptic detail, every unexplained behavior, every thread the player can pull — it traces back to something in here. Nothing in this game is random. Nothing is atmosphere for its own sake.

The player will never read this file. But every line of dialogue, every expedition log, every scrap of paper found in a dead zone was written by someone who did.

---

## The Shepherds

They have no name the outside world knows. Survivors who have found fragments of their existence call them different things — the Architects, the Old Hand, the Quiet. Inside, among themselves, they use a word that translates roughly as **the Shepherds.**

They are not a modern organization. They do not have a founding date in any conventional sense.

Their origin traces to the first organized human civilizations — the moment people built walls and hierarchies and decided some lives were worth more than others. A small group of people, even then, saw the pattern. They stepped back from power not to escape it but to stand above it. To watch. To intervene when the trajectory became unrecoverable. To reset when reset was the only remaining option.

They believe, with complete sincerity and absolute conviction, that humanity cannot govern itself. That left alone, people will always trend toward the same ending — war, consumption, collapse. They have watched it happen before. Not once. Several times across the full span of recorded and unrecorded history.

The floods in ancient memory. The plagues. The collapses of civilizations that historians still cannot fully explain. Not all of them were the Shepherds — some were just history being history. But some weren't.

They are not evil in their own understanding. They are gardeners. They prune because they love the garden.

They have been preparing Protocol Zero for over a century. The frequency research alone spans four generations of internal scientists. The infrastructure embedding — quietly built into global communications systems over decades — was the work of lifetimes. By the time it was ready, the people who started it were long dead. The people who executed it inherited a conviction they were raised inside of, a mission that had the weight of generations behind it, and a vote that passed by a narrow margin over the objection of a significant internal faction.

That margin matters. It's why the world is the way it is now.

## Protocol Zero

Not a weapon. Not a virus. A frequency.

Human cognition at its most complex — language, abstraction, long-term planning, ideology, empathy at scale — operates within a specific neurological bandwidth. The Shepherds spent generations identifying that bandwidth, mapping it, and developing a signal capable of disrupting it at a global scale simultaneously.

The intention was precise and, in their framework, humane: suppress the neurological capacity for civilization-level behavior. Leave the substrate. Leave the body that breathes and moves and responds to hunger and warmth. Nobody dies. They simply stop being the version of themselves capable of building weapons, starting wars, burning the world down.

A reset to something manageable. Something the Shepherds believed they could guide forward differently this time.

The transmission was embedded in global communications infrastructure and broadcast simultaneously worldwide. It lasted eleven seconds.

It worked. And it didn't.

## The Malfunction — Why the Altered Are Violent

The frequency was imprecise in a way the internal models did not predict.

Disrupting the higher cognitive bandwidth does not make it go quiet. It fragments it. And fragments of human intelligence — stripped of language, empathy, long-term consequence modeling, and social context — do not produce a simplified person. They produce something running on pure instinct and threat response with nothing left to moderate it.

The Altered are not empty. They are broken. Shards of a person, still processing, still reacting, with no framework left to interpret what they're processing or regulate how they react to it.

The violence is not a feature. It is a catastrophic malfunction that a significant internal faction warned about and was overruled.

That faction has not said *we told you so.* They are too busy trying to fix it.

## The Signal — Why It Still Spreads

The broadcast nodes are still running.

Not everywhere. But enough. Critical infrastructure that survived the collapse, still operating on backup power systems the Shepherds built to last decades without maintenance. The nodes continue to broadcast at a lower ambient level — not enough to cause immediate conversion, but enough that prolonged proximity matters.

Long-term exposure — weeks or months in range of an active node — can push a partially resistant person toward the threshold. It is slow. It is not inevitable for everyone. But it is why certain zones feel wrong after extended time. Why expedition teams that work The Crossing too long come back quieter. Why nobody lives in the Dead Zones anymore.

The survivors are drawn almost entirely from the one in fifty whose neurological makeup does not respond to the frequency. This is not random. There is a pattern to who resisted. The Shepherds have theories. The Repentant faction is quietly horrified by what those theories imply about what the original architects actually intended to preserve.

The Altered do not spread through contact. The world itself, in certain places, is still broadcasting.

## The Altered Behavior — Why They Organize

Near active broadcast nodes, Altered behavior changes.

They are not becoming more intelligent. They are orienting. The signal that broke them is still pulling at what remains of their cognition, and near its source that pull becomes strong enough to produce coordinated movement — not planned, not conscious, but coherent in a way that reads from a distance like organization.

The Altered cluster toward broadcast nodes. Near those nodes they stand still, face consistent directions, move in patterns. Three expeditions to The Yard reported them facing the same direction and could not agree on which direction. They were all correct — the Altered were facing the node, and the expeditions approached from different angles.

The node beneath the mall in The Crossing is directly below the second floor. That is why Mark's note says *no exceptions*. The Altered density above ground level is not incidental. They are drawn there. Standing. Waiting for something they cannot name and will never be able to articulate.

The markings on walls in the Gray Zones that no surviving human has claimed: they are not language. They are the Altered's fragmented cognition attempting to do something it once knew how to do. It means nothing. It also means something is still running in there, trying.

## Mark

Mark Delacroix. Coordinator of the Camp for fourteen months before his disappearance.

He was recruited by the Repentant faction eighteen months ago by a surviving member of the original Shepherd organization — an elderly man living in The Line who had voted against Protocol Zero and spent the years since trying to undo it.

Mark was not told everything. Nobody outside the inner circle gets everything. He was told what he needed to know: broadcast nodes exist, they are still running, the Altered clustering is a proximity effect, and shutting the nodes down is possible with the right access and knowledge. He was given a method for identifying node locations and a partial map of the ones already confirmed.

He accepted the role because he had lost people he loved to the Altered and the question of *why* had been eating him alive for four years. He was not an idealist. He was a man who needed the answer to one question and was willing to do dangerous things to get it.

He ran the Camp genuinely. The care was not performance. But some of his decisions as Coordinator were shaped by intelligence gathering — which zones to prioritize, which routes to map, which trades to pursue with which settlements. Bridget's private ledger has been tracking anomalies in his resource decisions without her understanding what she's tracking or why it matters.

The Committed faction identified him through a channel that still exists from Before — someone in the Camp reported an anomaly to the wrong person, not knowing what they were doing or who they were reporting to. The someone does not know what they caused. They may never know.

Mark is alive. Being held at a location outside the Camp's known zones. The Committed want the full node map — the locations the Repentant have already identified and confirmed. Mark has not given it. The notebook he left behind contains that map, encoded in a personal cipher built from references only someone who knew him well would recognize.

Nobody in the Camp has cracked it yet.

Whether Mark survives depends on whether the player finds the cipher before the Committed decide he has no more information to withhold.

## The Chapel — The Woman in the Lowfield

Her name is Vera. Former communications engineer, mid-fifties.

She was hired by a Shepherd front organization twenty years ago to help build and maintain global signal infrastructure. She was good at her job. She was not told what the infrastructure was for until eleven seconds of transmission made it impossible not to understand.

She has been living alone for four years with what she knows and what she helped build. She is not suicidal. She has decided that dying would be the easier thing and she does not deserve the easier thing.

She has been mapping broadcast nodes from the outside — identifying them by Altered clustering patterns, by ambient frequency she can measure with equipment she salvaged and modified, by the data she already had from her years of infrastructure work. She has a method for shutting one down. Possibly more than one. She has never tested it because testing it alone, without backup, in proximity to a node, is how she dies.

She has watched the Camp for months. She watched Mark and understood roughly what he was doing. She watched his disappearance. She has been watching the player since the first week of the new Coordinatorship.

She is deciding whether the Camp is worth trusting. Whether *you* are worth trusting.

She will make contact when something specific happens — not on a timer, not on a calendar. When you do something that answers the question she's been asking about you since she started watching.

She does not know what that thing is yet. She'll know it when she sees it.

## The Line — The Remnant Shepherds

The structures in The Line were built over forty years, completed a decade before Protocol Zero, as contingency infrastructure. Safe houses. Supply caches. Communication equipment designed to function without global networks. The Shepherds who voted against the reset built them, quietly, as a hedge against the possibility that the majority would eventually win the vote.

When Protocol Zero executed, eight members of the Repentant faction were already in The Line. They have been there since. Older now. Some dead of age and circumstance in the years since. Four remain.

They disagree about everything except one thing: what was done was wrong and if there is a way to correct it, that correction is their obligation.

The eldest of the four is the man who recruited Mark. He has not left The Line in two years — his health does not allow it. He knows Mark is taken. He does not have the resources to act on it alone. He has been waiting for the Camp to develop enough capacity to be worth approaching.

He is the only person alive who has the complete node map.

He is also the only person alive who knows what the Shepherds' internal records say about the one-in-fifty — the neurological pattern that resisted the frequency — and why that pattern is not random, and what it means about what the original architects of Protocol Zero actually designed the reset to preserve.

He has decided that information is the last card he holds. He will not give it away. He will trade it for something. He is still deciding what.

## The Truck in The Yard

It belongs to a woman named Cassian. Scout and field operative for a settlement called Redwall, located approximately forty kilometers northeast — outside the Camp's known world, outside any map the Camp currently has.

Redwall has pieced together more of the truth than the Camp has. Not the full picture — nobody has the full picture — but enough to understand that the Altered clustering is node-related and that the nodes can be shut down. They have been quietly building a coalition of settlements willing to attempt coordinated node deactivation. They sent Cassian to assess whether the Camp was worth bringing in.

Cassian went into The Yard to study the Altered clustering behavior near the node. She got too close. She is alive, hiding in the upper level of the eastern warehouse, running low on supplies, unable to leave without moving through a concentration of Altered that has grown since she arrived.

She has been there for nine days.

She has information about Redwall, about the coalition, and about two node locations Vera hasn't mapped yet. She will share all of it in exchange for extraction.

Whether the Camp finds her before day fourteen — when her supplies run out and her options narrow to one — is a function of how aggressively the player is running expeditions into The Yard and how carefully they're searching.

## What the Player Will Never Be Told Directly

- The name of the organization
- The full history of previous resets
- The complete node map
- What the one-in-fifty neurological pattern means and why it wasn't random
- Whether Mark is still alive at any given moment
- Who in the Camp reported the anomaly that got Mark taken
- What Vera decides about the player, and when

These things exist in the world. They can be inferred, partially reconstructed, approached from the edges. A player who pays attention across the full arc of the game will assemble something close to the truth.

They will never be certain they have it.

That's the point.
